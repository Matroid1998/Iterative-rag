A core component of most synthetic fertilizers.

Text:
Phosphorus (P) is an essential element for life that, along with nitrogen (N) and potassium, forms 
the core of most synthetic fertilizers. The demand for increased agricultural yields driven by the need to 
feed a growing population has resulted in global phosphorus fertilizer application tripling over the last 60 
years.1 However, modern phosphorus fertilizer production is unsustainable.
