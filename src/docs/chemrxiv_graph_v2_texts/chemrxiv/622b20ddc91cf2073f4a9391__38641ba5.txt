Description:
A substance whose consumption is a risk factor for breast cancer.

Text:
A variety of risk factors for breast cancer have been wellestablished by 
epidemiologic studies including family history of cancer, genetic traits, ethnicity, and race, as 
well as variable exposures of substances such as alcohol consumption, exogenous hormones, 
physical inactivity, and certain female reproductive factors 6. On the basis of molecular 
background, breast cancer is basically classified into three main subtypes i) Hormone 
receptorpositive estrogen receptor (ER); ii) human epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) 
2 (HER2)positive; and iii) triple negative tumors 7,8. Initially, it was assume that estrogen 
receptors are responsible to stimulate the growth of breast cancer 9.
