VOC buildup in indoor air is 
a general problem of widespread interest because of the potential for negative health effects and 
discomfort. Some common VOCs, including benzene and formaldehyde, are classified as carcinogenic to 
humans.57 VOCs are prevalent in buildings, homes and cars and pollute indoor spaces via offgassing from 
various materials,8,9 outdoor air pollution entering residential and occupational spaces (car emissions, 
engineering activities, geological activity, etc. )1012 and various indoor activities (cooking, use of cleaning 
agents, etc. ).13 VOC reduction is of great public interest as it will improve health and environmental 
conditions.
