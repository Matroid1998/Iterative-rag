Name: N-Methylaniline
Text:
N-methylaniline appears as a colorless to brown viscous liquid. Insoluble in water and denser than water. Contact may irritate skin, eyes and mucous membranes. May be toxic by ingestion. Used to make other chemicals.
N-methylaniline is a methylaniline that is aniline carrying a methyl substituent at the nitrogen atom. It is a phenylalkylamine, a secondary amine and a methylaniline. It is functionally related to an aniline.
N-Methylaniline has been reported in Camellia sinensis and Lupinus albus with data available.
