Name: Triethylaluminum
Text:
Aluminum triethyl appears as a colorless liquid. Flammable gas is produced on contact with water. (USCG, 1999)
Triethylaluminium (TEA) is an organoaluminium compound. It is used in various chemical processing and as an ignitor for jet and rocket engines. Aluminum is the most abundant metal in the earth's crust and is always found combined with other elements such as oxygen, silicon, and fluorine.  (L739, L740, L781)
