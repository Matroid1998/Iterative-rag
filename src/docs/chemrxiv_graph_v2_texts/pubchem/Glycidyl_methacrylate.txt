Glycidyl Methacrylate can cause cancer according to California Labor Code.
Glycidyl methacrylate is a colorless liquid with a fruity odor. Floats on water. (USCG, 1999)
Glycidyl methacrylate is an enoate ester obtained by formal condensation of the carboxy group of methacrylic acid with the hydroxy group of glycidol. It is an enoate ester and an epoxide. It is functionally related to a methacrylic acid and a glycidol.
