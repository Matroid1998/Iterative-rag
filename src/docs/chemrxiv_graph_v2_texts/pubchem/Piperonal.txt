Name: Piperonal
Text:
Piperonal appears as colorless lustrous crystals. (NTP, 1992)
Piperonal is an arenecarbaldehyde that is 1,3-benzodioxole substituted by a formyl substituent at position 5. It has been isolated from Piper nigrum. It has a role as a plant metabolite, a fragrance and an insect repellent. It is a member of benzodioxoles and an arenecarbaldehyde.
Piperonal has been reported in Rostrinucula dependens, Heliotropium lasiocarpum, and other organisms with data available.
Piperonal is a metabolite found in or produced by Saccharomyces cerevisiae.
See also: Black Pepper (part of).
