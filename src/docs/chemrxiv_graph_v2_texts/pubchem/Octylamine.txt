Name: Octylamine
Text:
Octanamine appears as a yellow liquid with an ammonia-like odor. Insoluble in water and less dense than water. Hence floats on water. Contact may irritate skin, eyes and mucous membranes. May be toxic by ingestion. Used to make other chemicals.
Octan-1-amine is an 8-carbon primary aliphatic amine. It has a role as a metabolite. It is a conjugate base of an octan-1-aminium.
Octylamine has been reported in Hippodamia convergens, Apis cerana, and other organisms with data available.
