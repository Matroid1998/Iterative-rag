Name: Calcium sulfate
Text:
Calcium sulfate appears as odorless, white powder or colorless, crystalline solid. Crystals sometimes have a blue, gray or reddish tinge or can be brick red. Density: 2.96 g cm-3.
Calcium sulfate is a calcium salt and an inorganic calcium salt.
Anhydrite is a mineral with formula of CaS6+O4 or Ca(SO4). The IMA symbol is Anh.
Gypsum is a mineral with formula of CaS6+O4·2H2O or Ca(SO4)·2H2O. The IMA symbol is Gp.
A calcium salt that is used for a variety of purposes including: building materials, as a desiccant, in dentistry as an impression material, cast, or die, and in medicine for immobilizing casts and as a tablet excipient. It exists in various forms and states of hydration. Plaster of Paris is a mixture of powdered and heat-treated gypsum.
