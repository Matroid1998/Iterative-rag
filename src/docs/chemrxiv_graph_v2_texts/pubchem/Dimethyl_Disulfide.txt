Name: Dimethyl Disulfide
Text:
Dimethyl disulfide appears as a colorless oily liquid with a garlic-like odor. Denser than water and slightly soluble in water. Vapors heavier than air. May irritate skin and eyes. Used to make other chemicals.
Dimethyl disulfide is an organic disulfide that is methane in which one of the hydrogens has been replaced by a methyldisulfanyl group. It has a role as a xenobiotic metabolite.
Dimethyl disulfide has been reported in Solanum tuberosum, Allium ursinum, and other organisms with data available.
